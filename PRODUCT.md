# Product

## Register

product

## Users

High school students and staff at Green Level High School. Students use it when they've lost something on campus (stressed, hoping to find it quickly) or found something and want to do the right thing. Administrators use it to manage submissions, review claims, and coordinate returns — typically from a desk during the school day.

## Product Purpose

A campus lost-and-found system that lets students report found items, search for missing belongings, submit ownership claims, and message about items. Administrators approve submissions, verify claims, and oversee the process. Success looks like: items get reunited with owners quickly, the process is transparent, and the school has a trustworthy system they're proud to show off — including at FBLA Nationals.

## Brand Personality

Trustworthy, Clean, Helpful. The voice is confident but approachable — like a well-organized school office, not a startup. No jargon, no hype. Direct and clear.

## Anti-references

- Generic school tools (Canvas, Schoology, PowerSchool) — functional but uninspiring, dated UI
- Over-designed SaaS dashboards with gratuitous gradients, glassmorphism, or dark-mode-by-default
- Anything that looks "obviously AI-generated" — cream/sand backgrounds, gradient text, identical card grids with numbered eyebrows
- Overly corporate enterprise software with dense tables and gray everything

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity over cleverness** — Every element earns its place. If it doesn't help someone find or return an item, it doesn't belong.
2. **Trust through craft** — Polish signals reliability. A well-built interface tells users the system behind it is dependable.
3. **Quiet confidence** — The design should feel assured, not loud. Premium without being flashy.
4. **Speed to resolution** — Reduce friction at every step. The fastest path from "I lost something" to "I got it back" wins.
5. **Show, don't tell** — Demonstrate competence through the interface itself, not through marketing copy about how great the system is.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA minimum
- All interactive elements keyboard-accessible
- Sufficient color contrast (4.5:1 body text, 3:1 large text)
- Reduced motion alternatives for all animations
- Clear focus indicators
